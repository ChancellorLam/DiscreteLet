Introduction To Logic:

Logic is the study of valid reasoning, argumentation, and deduction. Key areas of study include the fundamental laws of logic (like identity and non-contradiction), meaning of symbols, structure of arguments, and the relationship between logic and truth. Logic is a foundational element to philosophy, mathematics as well as computer science.

I. Logical Symbols

Propositional logic is considered to be the most basic form of logic. It serves as a foundational system that studies the logical relationships of propositions/statements, which are treated as units that are either true or false. It forms the basis of more complex logical systems like first-order-logic. Propositional logic deals with the truth values of propositions and how they combine using logical connectives.
